The Reflection of Sadness

By BAABUSHA KOHLI

What boils each instant like tea on the stove
is not sadness—
But merely the tingling of sadness

Like the sharp prick of a needle
piercing skin and tearing through flesh
Pain takes its own long time to commence—
It nests in the veins for days
and keeps blood tepid

Sadness has no image,
only a reflection:
of a face buried in hands shrinking from the mirror
The sharp transparency of the mirror penetrates it—
Sadness doesn’t flow out
but congeals like blood clots

Sadness erupts after ages
with the slow soft caress of someone’s fingertips—
Sadness drips ever so slowly

Sadness explodes when the moon jumps to its death in the river
leaving behind its bright reflection in the sky
And the poet leans a ladder against the sky
climbs up high
and steals the moon’s reflection—
While the moon’s corpse melts away in the river
